2020-10-28

Published Wed 28 October 2020 in Fiction

by David O'Dwyer

"Welcome to the worst casino in the continental United States!"

It wasn't a reassuring greeting but it was certainly an honest one. A man in a polyester pinstripe suit wearing a garish white top hat greeted the party at the door, holding a Party City brand cane. Candice turned to her partner and whispered, "Where did you bring me."
"The worst casino, of course." Juniper was beaming from ear to ear. She shook her head slightly to accentuate the swinging of earrings made of dice. "It's awful, I cannot wait. Entrance greeter man, where's the check-in counter?"
The man took the disrespect with the grace of someone without dignity. "It's over to the right, behind the column so it's really hard to find."
"Your right or my right?"
"Oh, my right, so your left."
"Thought so." Juniper gave Candice a reassuring smile that reassured no one. They carried out the business of checking in, getting their keys, taking an elevator, and arriving at their room.

The room was a masterpiece of disappointment. There was a single bed that was advertised as a queen but was definitely a twin. The television was a CRT, a bulky thing; it looked like it even had an integrated VCR. Light filtered in through the yellowing curtains; the reduced light in the room helped to hide the stains on the shag carpet flooring and the water stains on the walls. It lived up to the casino's reputation.

"This is surprisingly good," murmured Juniper, as she toured around the room opening up every drawer she could find.
"Oh, great," Candice said. She had intended it to be sarcastic but it didn't land.
"I know, right? Found the Bible, lemme see." Juniper flipped through it. "Someone drew some dicks in here. A lot of them, and in pen. I don't even think this room has pens."
Candice had put down her bags and started unpacking. She barely registered Juniper pulling the carpet up off the floor, but she did register it.
"Juniper what the FUCK are you doing," acknowledged Candice.
"Picked this room for a reason, love. Look, trapdoor." She was right - underneath the carpet was wooden flooring and a circle-shaped door. And she immediately pulled it open. "Could you grab the flashlights from my bag?"
"I will not. Juniper, what is that? Why is it in our room?"
"Trapdoor? Because they built it here?"
Well, she had her there. Candice furrowed her brows and frowned at Juniper, who had gotten up and begun rummaging through her luggage.
"Okay, you're right. Sorry Candice. Should have mentioned it sooner." Juniper was attaching a very large knife to her belt.
"This isn't a holiday, is it?"
"Nope. Sorry love, you probably should have guessed though. Did you not google the place?"
"Uhm. Why?"
"It's crazy haunted here. Many suicides. People usually think that it's the shittiness of the place but we know better, don't we?"
"Whatever. Hand me the body armor."
"Couldn't find your gun, is that okay?"
"No, I brought that one myself. Can't trust you at all." She gave Juniper an insincere smile.

A dropped glowstick fell for a few seconds before landing about 50 feet down. They hammered in some stakes to attach a rope ladder, put on some headlamps, and began their journey down.
